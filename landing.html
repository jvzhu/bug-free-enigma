Security you can verify

Every line of the crypto implementation is open source. No black boxes. No trust required.

  • PBKDF2 — 100,000 iterations + random salt. Your password is never stored.
  • HKDF — Key expansion with domain separation. One master key → per-purpose keys.
  • AES-GCM-256 — Authenticated encryption. Each note has a unique random IV.
  • WebAuthn — Hardware-backed biometric login. Biometric data never leaves your device.

Frequently asked questions

Can Bug-Free Enigma read my notes?

No. The encryption key is derived from your password in your browser and is never transmitted anywhere. We have no backend and no way to access your data.

What happens if I forget my master password?

You can use one of the 10 single-use recovery codes generated when you set up your password. Without a code, encrypted notes cannot be recovered — which is by design.

Is biometric login secure?

Yes. WebAuthn biometrics uses hardware-backed private keys that never leave your device or browser. Your fingerprint data is processed entirely by your device's secure enclave.

Does it work offline?

Yes — once loaded, the app works completely offline. Notes are stored in your browser's localStorage.

Is the source code available?

Absolutely. Bug-Free Enigma is fully open source under the MIT licence on GitHub. Inspect every line of the crypto implementation.

What encryption algorithm does it use?

AES-GCM-256 for encryption, PBKDF2 (100,000 iterations, random salt) for key derivation, and HKDF for key expansion. All via the browser's native Web Crypto API (crypto.subtle).
